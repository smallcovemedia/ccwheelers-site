Campfires

Campfires at Oceano Dunes

Yes, you can have a real driftwood fire on the sand here, at one of the last beaches in California where that's still true. Here's how to do it right, keep it legal, and keep the next camper's feet unburned.

The Rules

Keep It Legal

  • Where: campfires are allowed on the sand south of Grand Avenue, between the shoreline and the western edge of the foredunes. No fires on the boardwalk side, in vegetated dune areas, or within 50 feet of a restroom or picnic table.
  • Size limit: keep it to 3 feet across and 2 feet tall. Bonfire-sized burns draw rangers, not compliments.
  • Wood only. Clean, untreated wood. Pallets and boards with nails, screws, or any metal are prohibited, illegal to even bring onto the beach, and will earn you a ticket. Twisted metal in the sand becomes a tire and foot hazard long after your fire is gone.
  • No trash in the fire. Burning anything other than wood is against the law.
  • Hours: fires have to be out by 10 PM unless you're registered, paid-up camping south of Post 2. Day use fires and unregistered camps go dark at 10 PM, no exceptions.
  • Mind the wind. It can shift in a moment. Keep serious distance between your fire and anything with a gas tank: campers, quads, fuel cans.
Rules change with conditions During high fire danger, State Parks can add restrictions. Confirm at the entrance kiosk when you arrive, or call the ranger station at (805) 473-7220. Ask them directly: "Any fire restrictions in effect today?" Ten seconds at the gate beats finding out the hard way at 9 PM with a truck full of firewood.
The One Rule That Matters Most

Never Bury a Fire in Sand

Sand insulates. Coals buried under it stay scorching hot for hours, sometimes into the next day, invisible to the kid running barefoot to the water or the rider dropping a tire into your old pit. Visible is safe. Hidden is not.

  • Let it burn out. Rangers and park staff actually prefer this to someone trying to hide or bury a fire fast before they leave. A visible fire or ash pile is obvious and gets avoided. A buried one isn't, and that's the real danger.
  • Dousing works too. If you want it out sooner, water beats sand every time: douse it, stir it, douse it again until it's cool to the touch. Never smother it under sand to make it disappear.
  • Bring jugs either way. The same water jugs locals carry for getting unstuck do double duty at the fire pit if you want to speed things along.
Firewood

Where the Wood Comes From

🌊

Free Driftwood

Unlike most state parks, Oceano Dunes encourages collecting the driftwood laying around, and after a winter storm there's plenty. Check the high-tide line and the creek mouth. Storm weeks are firewood jackpots.

🚚

Delivered to Camp

Vendor trucks patrol the camping beach selling firewood, ice, water, and snacks right to your site. Flag one down when it rolls by; beach prices beat breaking camp for a bundle of wood.

🏪

On the Way In

Grab bundles with your beer and ice at the last stops: Pier Liquor on Pier Avenue or the Grand Avenue stores in Grover Beach. Details in the supplies guide.

Planning a trip? The Trip Planner builds your fire nights into a full plan: tide-timed arrival, camp setup, gear checklist, and all. And check wind conditions before fire night: over 20 mph and your fire becomes everyone's problem.
